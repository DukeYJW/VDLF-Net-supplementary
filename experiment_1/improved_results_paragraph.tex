\textbf{Results on CIFAR-100.} As shown in Table \ref{tab:standard_results}, 
on the fine-grained CIFAR-100 dataset, our VDLF-Net achieves the best performance 
across all evaluation metrics: an accuracy of \textbf{57.29\%}, precision of 
\textbf{57.57\%}, recall of \textbf{57.29\%}, and F1-score of \textbf{57.15\%}. 
Compared to the baseline models, VDLF-Net outperforms ResNet-50 Enhanced by 
\textbf{1.46 percentage points} in accuracy (57.29\% vs. 55.83\%) and 
\textbf{1.44 percentage points} in F1-score (57.15\% vs. 55.71\%). 
Furthermore, VDLF-Net shows substantial improvements over VGG-16, with gains of 
\textbf{7.35 percentage points} in accuracy and \textbf{7.28 percentage points} in F1-score. 
The consistent superiority across all four metrics (accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score) 
demonstrates the effectiveness of our model's adaptive feature mechanism and variational 
regularization in enhancing representation learning, particularly in distinguishing fine-grained 
categories. Notably, the precision score of 57.57\% indicates that VDLF-Net effectively reduces 
false positives, while the balanced precision-recall performance (57.57\% vs. 57.29\%) suggests 
robust classification across all 100 classes. These results validate the synergistic integration 
of deep representation learning with variational inference, showcasing how the feature-adaptive 
approximation mechanism enables more discriminative representations compared to standalone 
deep networks.
